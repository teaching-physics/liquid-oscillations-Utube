Damped oscillations of a liquid in a U tube

This is the simulation of the damped motion of a fluid in a vertical U-tube with density ρ. The motion is restricted to the vertical dimension y. (We choose a coordinate system in the plane with origin at the fixed equilibrium level of the fluid, which depends on the amount of liquid).

We have assumed that the reaction of the fluid to a displacement dy from the equilibrium point can be modeled using Newton's law for a constant mass situation, ma = ΣFi , where Fi is the force exerted by gravity and the friction related to the tube walls. Thus, applying Newton's second law, we obtain the following second-order ordinary differential equation:

m d2y / dt2 = -2gy/L-bvy ,

where y is the horizontal position of the liquid end in the tube.

In the simulation we solve numerically this equation and visualize the results.

The simulation allows changing the different parameters that appear in the equation of motion, such as the height and cross-sectional area of the tube, the density of the liquid and the damping constant.
